SITUATION SUMMARY

As of end-2023, Nigeria's economy presents a bifurcated picture: real GDP growth
held at 3.5% in Q4 2023, sustained by a resilient non-oil private sector — yet
headline inflation breached 28.9%, driven by the June 2023 petrol subsidy removal
and naira float that depreciated the exchange rate from NGN 467 to over NGN 907
per USD within six months. This combination of moderate growth and accelerating
inflation represents the single most critical risk to local-currency-denominated
portfolio returns in the near term.

PRIORITY RECOMMENDATIONS

1. HEDGE NAIRA EXPOSURE ON ALL LOCAL-CURRENCY POSITIONS [HIGH PRIORITY]
   Exchange rate is the statistically dominant driver of headline inflation
   (OLS coefficient: 0.026 per NGN/USD, p<0.001, Adj R²=0.63). Prophet model
   12-month forecast projects continued inflationary pressure at 29–34% through
   mid-2024, contingent on further FX depreciation. Recommendation: restructure
   at least 60% of NGN-denominated instruments into USD-linked or indexed
   instruments within 90 days. Estimated impact: preserves ~15–20pp of real
   return versus unhedged exposure.

2. OVERWEIGHT NON-OIL SECTORS — TELECOM, FINTECH, AGRIBUSINESS [HIGH PRIORITY]
   Non-oil GDP growth (mean: 1.83% quarterly) consistently outpaces oil-sector
   growth (mean: −0.59%) across 2015–2023, with post-COVID recovery averaging
   3.14% quarterly (vs 1.29% pre-COVID, paired t-test p=0.005, Cohen's d=1.04).
   SHAP analysis of state-level economic predictors confirms that urban
   connectivity (internet penetration, urban population share) are the strongest
   positive structural drivers. Recommendation: allocate new capital into
   fintech infrastructure, digital agriculture, and logistics sectors where
   non-oil growth fundamentals are strongest. Target implementation: 6-month
   deployment window, Q1–Q2 2024.

3. PRIORITISE SOUTHERN STATES FOR DEPLOYMENT — AVOID NORTH-EAST, NORTH-WEST
   WITHOUT RISK PREMIUM [MEDIUM PRIORITY]
   One-way ANOVA confirms statistically significant unemployment inequality
   across geopolitical zones (F=10.65, p<0.001, η²=0.63 large effect).
   North-West (avg: 41.7%) and North-East (avg: 41.0%) unemployment rates are
   18pp higher than South-West (avg: 23.3%) and South-South (avg: 23.6%).
   Additionally, 100% of Northern zone-quarters classify as 'high informal'
   (>90% informal employment, χ²=48.0, p<0.001, Cramér's V=1.0), indicating
   weaker formal market infrastructure for investment deployment. Recommendation:
   weight new project financing toward Lagos, Rivers, and South-West states;
   apply a 300–400bp risk premium for Northern-state exposures.

INFLATION OUTLOOK

The Prophet model's 12-month forecast (MAPE=12.2%) projects inflation remaining
above 25% through H1 2024 before moderating toward 22–24% by Q4 2024, assuming
no further CBN rate adjustments or additional naira depreciation. For local-
currency investment returns, a 25%+ inflation environment implies a real return
hurdle rate of at least 30% NGN-denominated yield to generate positive USD-
equivalent returns — a threshold currently met by select FGN Eurobonds and
high-yield infrastructure notes, but not by traditional equity or real estate.

Baseline GDP forecast for 2024–2025 is 4.0% annual growth; downside scenario
(oil at $55/barrel, naira −20%) implies ~1.8% growth — a $16.0B USD GDP
differential between scenarios that directly informs infrastructure pipeline
sizing and disbursement timing decisions.
